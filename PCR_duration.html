In Eurosurveillance 2020 Jul 30; 25(30): 2001292, Omar et al. analysed data of consecutive RT-qPCR results in 537 symptomatic patients with mild COVID-19 in home quarantine, between 28 February to 6 June 2020 in Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany. Table 1 presents their reported data on the population with an RT-qPCR positive for SARS-CoV-2 RNA at mean (95%CI) days after symptom onset.

Simulated study population

For 1000 times, at each iteration of time t, sample n random numbers from a normal distribution with mean = mu and sd = sigma, which replicates the upper and lower bounds of observed time.

This random draw averages parameters for a gamma distribution across 1,000 simulated populations, which approximates the distribution from Omar et al.
